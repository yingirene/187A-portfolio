Project: Music App Redesign

Date: March 2016

Redesign of a Music Streaming App

Completed for the course COGS187a: Cognitive Aspects of Digital Design
Team members: Rafael Aguayo and Jeff Hoang
  • Week 1: Analyzing the Users
    • Interview college students on how they used music apps.
    • Recorded the interviewees walking through different scenarios.
    • For a variety of aspects, compared a set of apps.
      • Apps compared included: Spotify, Soundcloud, and YouTube.
      • Compared steps involved for different scenarios.
      • Compared the organization and interfaces of the different apps.
  • Week 2: Redesigning the Apps
    • Generated an initial design idea and direction.
    • Drew out thumbnails to plan the scenarios for the prototype we were making.
    • Created digital graphical assets to be used in the prototype.
      • Used GIMP for asset creation.
    • Created screen images to use with Invision.
  • I learned about how users interacted with various apps.
    • Users tailor their behaviors to the app and they prioritize the functionality over organization of the app.
    • Users usually know what they want and just want to directly get to their goal.
      • My group's app reflected this by focusing on the main functionalities and making the other tasks as straightforward as possible.
  • It was hard to decide on how we could improve on the existing designs used by the top apps in this category despite doing analyses on them.
    • We decided to aim our design towards improving the scenarios that the other apps had poor interactions for.
  • The final redesign aims to make scenarios, that the other apps were weaker with, a lot simpler. We emphasized the main functionality in the navigation and organization of the app in order to focus on the main needs of our users.
